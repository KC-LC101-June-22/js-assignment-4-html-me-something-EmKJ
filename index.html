Homemade instant oatmeal is better

Packaged oatmeal is sometimes pricey, sometimes too sweet and often times glue-y. My favorite instant oatmeal recipe adds in flax and chia for more complex flavor, better texture and bonus healthy stuff!

The case for savory oatmeal

What if you don't like sweet food? What if you just want to mix things up a bit in your kitchen?

Most of the recipes and pre packaged oatmeal I see are sweet. Oatmeal can be savory too! Try adding veggies or cheese, cooking with stock instead of water, and even topping with a fried egg. Yum~

Homemade Instant Oatmeal

Ingredients

  • 4 Cups + 1 Cup - Rolled Oats(Divided)
  • 1/6 Cup (17g) - Ground Flax
  • 1/4 Cup (35g) - Chia Seeds
  • 1 Tablespoon - Salt
  • Freeze Dried, Frozen or Fresh Fruit(Optional)
  • Pecans, Sliced Almonds or English Walnuts(optional)

Instructions

  1. Using a blender, food processor or spice grinder, process 1 cup oats until powdery.
  2. Add all oats, chia, flax and salt to an airtight container and shake to combine.

To Serve

  1. Add 1/3 cup instant oatmeal mix and 1 cup water to a microwave safe container. Add fruit, if using.
  2. Stir well.
  3. Microwave on high for 3 minutes. Watch to make sure it doesn't boil over!
  4. Top with cream, maple syrup and nuts.

Notes:

  • Freeze dried fruits travel really well.
  • You can pre-portion this recipe into baggies or mason jars.
  • I like to pack up 1/3 cup of oatmeal mix with a handful of freeze dried strawberries in a 1 pint mason jar to take with me to work.
  • The flavor combinations are endless: apple-cinnamon-pecan, blueberry-vanilla, peanut butter, nutella, banana/almond. A quick google search might turn up a new favorite!
