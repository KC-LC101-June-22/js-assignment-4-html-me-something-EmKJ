Do you think oats are boring?

Oats

Breakfast Flavored Glue?

Oats are a great fit for a small budget. They are low effort and quick to cook when life gets busy. They're also healthy: good for your heart, gut and blood-sugar. They can help with weight loss and...on their own they are not much different from eating cardboard.

I hope I can inspire you to add oats to your meals by showing you my favorite recipes (that arent glue-y or boring).

oatmeal

Homemade instant oatmeal

Packaged oatmeal is sometimes pricey, sometimes too sweet and often times glue-y. My favorite instant oatmeal recipe adds in flax and chia for more complex flavor, better texture and bonus healthy stuff!

healthy stuff +1

Then promptly adds in maple syrup and cream so it's not too healthy.

Add a pinch of salt. Adding salt can make sweet foods taste better. Because science!

The case for savory oatmeal

What if you don't like sweet food? What if you just want to mix things up a bit in your kitchen? Most of the recipes and pre packaged oatmeal I see are sweet. Oatmeal can be savory too! Try adding veggies or cheese, cooking with stock instead of water, and even topping with a fried egg. Yum~

Cookies

Oatmeal lace cookies are just oatmeal held together with caramel. They're crispy around the edges, chewy in the center and so simple to make.

Buy a digital scale and ditch your measuring cups. Using a scale lets you be sure that you've followed the recipe exactly right. You'll also (probably)make less dirty dishes and work faster! Need more convincing? Check out this article by Wirecutter.

Bread

Here's one of my go-to bread recipes. It's only 7 ingredients and 4 steps. Start to finish it takes about 10 minutes of hands-on time. The addition of oatmeal makes this bread turn out super soft, just like store bought bread.

I like to substitute the honey for a 50/50 sugar/molasses mix. The molasses addition makes the bread similar in taste to the brown bread served at places like Cheesecake Factory and Outback Steakhouse.
